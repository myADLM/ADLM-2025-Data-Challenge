MISCELLANEOUS MML REFERRAL TEST 3
1. PURPOSE:
The purpose of this protocol is to outline the procedures for the
analytical phase of generating results for Miscellaneous MML
Referral Test 3, ensuring accuracy, reliability, and compliance with
regulatory requirements.
Responsibility: Designated laboratory staff will perform the analysis of
Miscellaneous MML Referral Test 3 following the outlined procedures.
It is the responsibility of the staff to ensure all steps are executed
properly and to report any anomalies or discrepancies to the
supervisor.
1. SPECIMEN REQUIREMENTS:
Specimen Type:
• As specified by the referring laboratory.
Specimen Volume:
• As specified by the referring laboratory.
Specimen Stability:
• Specimen stability conditions (e.g., temperature, time) as outlined
by the referring laboratory must be adhered to.
Unacceptable Specimens:
• Specimens that do not comply with the referring laboratory's
requirements, including insufficient volume, improper storage
conditions, or exceeding stability timeframes.
1. EQUIPMENT, REAGENTS, AND SUPPLIES:
• As specified by the referring laboratory.
1. PROCEDURE:
A. Specimen Receipt:
• Verify the integrity of the specimen upon receipt.
• Ensure the specimen is properly labeled and matches the
documentation provided.
• Log the specimen into the laboratory information system (LIS).
B. Preparation and Analysis:
• Follow the analytical steps as specified by the referring
laboratory’s protocols:
1. Prepare the specimen as outlined in the referring laboratory
protocol (e.g., centrifugation, aliquoting).
2. Perform the assay using the designated equipment and
reagents, adhering strictly to the methodological instructions
provided.
3. Document the start time of the analysis and any pertinent
observations.
C. Quality Control:
• Perform quality control procedures as specified by the referring
laboratory:
1. Utilize appropriate control material provided by the referring
laboratory.
2. Conduct the analysis on control samples alongside patient
specimens.
3. Record and verify control results, ensuring they fall within
the established acceptable ranges.
D. Result Documentation and Verification:
• Review and verify the results obtained from the assay.
• Ensure results are accurately recorded and cross-verified with
control samples.
• If the results are outside the expected range, perform
troubleshooting steps as outlined by the referring laboratory.
• Validate the results in the LIS, including signing off for verification
by the responsible technologist.
E. Reporting Results:
• Transmit results to the referring laboratory as per their specified
format and guidelines.
• Ensure any critical findings are communicated promptly to the
referring laboratory.
F. Troubleshooting:
• In the event of assay failure or discrepancies in results:
1. Reassess the specimen integrity and preparation.
2. Review equipment calibration and reagent validity.
3. Repeat the assay if necessary.
4. Document any issues and actions taken, informing the
supervisor immediately.

1. QUALITY CONTROL:
• Adhere to internal and external quality control guidelines.
• Perform routine QC checks as specified.
• Validate equipment and reagents regularly, maintaining
documentation of QC procedures.
1. METHOD LIMITATIONS:
• Refer to referring laboratory's protocol and manufacturer's insert
for limitations pertaining to the specific assay.
• Ensure awareness of any potential interferences or factors
affecting assay accuracy.
