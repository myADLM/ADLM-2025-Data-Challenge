# PERFORMANCE DATA:

Performance testing f the ETI-MAX 3000™ consisted of running three FDA previously cleared Immunology assays by manual method as well as on the ETI-MAX $3 0 0 0 ^ { \top \mathsf { M } }$ to evaluate analytical sensitivity, linearity, reproducibility/precision, carry-over, and method comparison/ correlation.

Analytical sensitivity - Analytical sensitivty was determined for hemanual assays and assays on the ET1-MAX 3000™ by following NCCLS guideline EP-17A, Protocols for Determination of Limits of Detection and Limits of Quantitation.

Linearity - Linearity was established by testing serial dilutions of four positive sera across the full assay range for each product. The results indicated the ETl-MAX $3 0 0 0 ^ { \tau m }$ interpretations (positive, equivocal or negative) were within $+ 1 - 1$ dilution from the interpretations for the same dilutions using the manual method.

Reproducibility/Precision for semi-quantitive and/or qualitative samples - The instrument reproducibility/precision study was conducted at two external US laboratories and at DiaSorin Inc in Stillwater MN. A coded panel comprised of 8 frozen repository serum samples was prepared at DiaSorin and provided to each site for testing by the DiaSorin ETI-MAX 3000™M instrument and the three autoimmune assays. The panel contained 6 samples near the cut-off. Each site ran the coded samples using 4 replicates per run in 1 run per day over 5 days of operation (NCCLS: EP5-A2). Three different instruments were used. The results are summarized in the tables below as sample overall mean S/CO, %CVs computed for within run, between run, total, and instrument to instrument.
