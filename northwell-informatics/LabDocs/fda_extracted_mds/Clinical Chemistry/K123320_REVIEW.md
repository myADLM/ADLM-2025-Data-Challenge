# L. Test Principle:

The Dimension $^ \mathrm { \textregistered }$ Ammonia assay (AMM) is an enzymatic method that uses glutamate dehydrogenase (GLDH) and a stabilized NADPH analog. Ammonia reacts with $\mathfrak { a }$ - ketoglutarate and reduced cofactor to form L-glutamate and the cofactor. The reaction is catalyzed by glutamate dehydrogenase. The decrease in absorbance due to the oxidation of the reduced cofactor is monitored at $3 4 0 / 7 0 0 \mathrm { n m }$ and is proportional to the ammonia concentration.

# d. Detection limit:

The Limit of Detection (LoD) for AMM is $1 1 \ \mu \mathrm { g / d L }$ , determined consistent with CLSI guideline EP17-A 11 and with proportions of false positives $( { \mathfrak { a } } )$ less than $5 \%$

and false negatives (β) less than $5 \%$ ; based on 96 determinations, with 4 blank and 4 low level samples. The Limit of Blank (LoB) is $5 \mu \mathrm { g } / \mathrm { d L }$ .

LoD is the lowest concentration of analyte that can be detected reliably. LoB is the highest concentration that is likely to be observed for a blank sample.

The Limit of Quantitation (LoQ) for AMM is $1 7 \mu \mathrm { g / d L }$ determined consistent with CLSI Guideline EP17-A and an inter-assay imprecision of $< 2 0 \%$ Coefficient of Variation (CV).
