# L. Test Principle:

The FastPack® IP Sex Hormone Binding Globulin Immunoassay is a chemiluminescent paramagnetic particle, two-site sandwich immunoassay that employs specific monoclonal antibodies. A mixture of biotinylated anti-SHBG specific antibody and anti-SHBG antibody labeled with alkaline phosphatase reacts with SHBG from the patient’s sample forming a sandwich complex in the mixture. Streptavidin-coated paramagnetic particles are added to the reaction mixture. Wash steps remove unbound materials. Chemiluminogenic substrate is added to the bound complex and the chemiluminescence signal (relative luminescence units) is measured. The amount of relative luminescence units is directly proportional to the concentration of SHBG in the sample.

# b. Linearity/assay reportable range:

The linearity study was conducted following CLSI EP06-A guideline. A high serum sample pool with a value $> 1 8 0 \ \mathrm { n m o l / L }$ SHBG was intermixed with a low serum sample pool to make 11 concentrations spanning the intended linear range of the assay. All samples were run in quadruplicate determinations versus one FastPack® reagent lot on one FastPack® analyzer. The following linearity regression equation was obtained:

$$
\mathbf { y } { = } 0 . 9 8 6 1 \mathbf { x } + 0 . 4 7 8 7 ; \mathbf { r } ^ { 2 } = 0 . 9 9 9 3
$$

The results of this study demonstrate that this method is linear for a measuring range of 0.8 to $1 7 4 \mathrm { n m o l / L }$ .

# d. Detection limit:

Detection capability studies were conducted per CLSI EP17-A2 guideline.

For the limit of blank (LoB) study a total of 180 replicate determinations of a blank sample were run over 4 days on 6 FastPack $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ analyzers using three lots of FastPack $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ reagents. Data was analyzed using the non-parametric approach per CLSI EP17-A2 guideline. Based on the results of the study, the LoB was set to $0 . 0 8 \mathrm { n m o l / L }$ .

The limit of detection (LoD) study was conducted by testing 4 low-level SHBG samples over 4 days on 6 FastPack $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ analyzers using three lots of FastPack $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ reagents. LoD was determined based on the lowest amount of analyte in a sample that can be detected with type I and II error rates set to $5 \%$ . Based on the results of the study, the LoD was set to $0 . 2 0 \mathrm { n m o l / L }$ .

The limit of quantitation (LoQ) study was conducted by testing 4 low-level samples over 4 days on 6 FastPack $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ analyzers using three lots of FastPack $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ reagents. The LoQ was defined as the lowest concentration wherein a $< 2 0 \%$ CV occurs. The LoQ was calculated for each lot of reagents, and the LoQ that met the acceptance criteria at the representative performance reagent combination was selected. Based on the analysis of the study results the LoQ is set at $0 . 8 0 \mathrm { n m o l / L }$ .

The sponsor’s claimed measuring range is $0 . 8 { - } 1 7 4 ~ \mathrm { n m o l / L }$

# HAMA interference:

For the human anti-mouse antibodies (HAMA) interference, two serum sample pools, one containing ${ \sim } 1 0 ~ \mathrm { n m o l / L }$ SHBG and the second containing ${ \sim } 7 0 ~ \mathrm { n m o l / L }$ SHBG were prepared. Aliquots of the samples were spiked with concentrations up to 4 $\mu \mathrm { g / m L }$ HAMA and run against one lot of FastPack $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ reagents on one FastPack $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ analyzer in triplicate determinations. In addition, six patient samples (3 HBV-3 HIV false positive samples which represent specimens with high levels of heterophilic antibodies) were spiked with either saline (as a control) or heterophilic blocking reagent to determine the effect of the heterophile activity in relation to the saline control. The sponsor defines significant interference as $\pm 1 0 \%$ bias as compared to the control results. Based on the results of these studies, the FastPack $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ IP Sex Hormone Binding Globulin Immunoassay did not show HAMA interference up to 4 $\mu \mathrm { g / m L }$ or to the additional heterophile antibodies tested.

# High-dose hook effect:

To evaluate the potential for high dose hook effect on the FastPack $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ IP Sex Hormone Binding Globulin Immunoassay, samples were prepare to generate SHBG concentrations values of 2500, 1000, and $4 0 0 \mathrm { n m o l / L }$ . Each sample was assayed in triplicate determinations against one FastPack $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ reagent lot on two FastPack $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ analyzers. The results demonstrate that the FastPack $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ IP Sex Hormone Binding Globulin Immunoassay displays no hook effect up to and including 1000 nmol/L SHBG.
