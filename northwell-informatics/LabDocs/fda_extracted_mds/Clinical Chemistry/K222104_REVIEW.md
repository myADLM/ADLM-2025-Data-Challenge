# B Indication(s) for Use:

The Atellica $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ CH Diazo Total Bilirubin (D_TBil) assay is for in vitro diagnostic use in the quantitative determination of total bilirubin in adults and children (non-neonates) in human serum and plasma using the Atellica $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ CH Analyzer. Measurement of total bilirubin, an organic compound formed during the normal and abnormal destruction of red blood cells, is used in the diagnosis and treatment of liver, hemolytic, hematological, and metabolic disorders, including hepatitis and gall bladder block.

# IV Device/System Characteristics:

# A Device Description:

The Atellica $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ CH Diazo Total Bilirubin (D_TBil) assay consists of the following 2 packs:   
Pack 1 (P1) $2 3 . 5 \mathrm { m L }$ Phosphate buffer (50mmol/L); NaCl (150mmol/L)   
Pack 2 (P2) 8.8ml 2,4-Dicholoroaniline (5mmol/L); HCl (130mmol/L); Na-Nitrite (0.5mmol/L)

Materials required but not provided: Atellica CH Bilirubin Calibrator (BILI CAL).

# B Principle of Operation:

The Atellica $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ CH Diazo Total Bilirubin (D_TBil) is an assay that uses 2,4-dichloroaniline (DCA). Direct bilirubin in the presence of diazotized DCA forms a red-colored azocompound in acidic solution that can be measured. A specific mixture of detergents enables the determination of total bilirubin. The detection wavelength is $5 4 5 \mathrm { n m }$ .

This assay requires $6 . 0 \mu \mathrm { L }$ of sample for a single determination.

2. Linearity:

Linearity studies were conducted according to CLSI-EP06 $2 ^ { \mathrm { n d } }$ Edition. A dilution series composed of 9 levels $( 0 . 0 4 - 2 7 . 9 1 \mathrm { m g / d L } )$ was prepared by mixing different proportions of the high serum pool sample and the low serum pool sample. Four replicates for each sample were measured on the Atellica CH Analyzer using 3 reagent lots. The data was analyzed using a linear regression model as described in the guideline. The results support the claimed measuring range of $0 . 1 0 \mathrm { m g } / \mathrm { d L } - 2 5 . 0 \mathrm { m g } / \mathrm { d L }$ .

5. Traceability, Stability, Expected Values (Controls, Calibrators, or Methods): The assay is traceable to SRM916 (NIST) Standard Reference Material.

6. Detection Limit:

Limit of Blank (LoB), Limit of Detection (LoD) and Limit of Quantitation (LoQ) were validated based on the CLSI guideline EP17-A2.

To calculate the LoB, four blank samples in replicates of five were tested using three reagent lots on one Atellica CH Analyzer over three days for a total of 60 measurements per reagent lot. LoB was determined using the $9 5 \%$ nonparametric percentile of the replicates for each of three reagent lots. The claimed LoB is $0 . 0 1 \mathrm { m g / d L }$ .

To calculate the LoD, two low native serum samples and two samples prepared from diluted serum pools were tested in replicates of five on one Atellica CH Analyzer using three reagent lots over three days for a total of 60 measurements per lot. The maximum observed LoD across the 3 lots tested was $0 . 0 2 \mathrm { m g / d L }$ . The claimed LoD is $0 . 0 2 \mathrm { m g / d L }$ .

To calculate the LoQ, five low level total bilirubin sample pools targeted between 0.03 $\mathrm { m g / d L }$ and $0 . 1 7 \mathrm { m g / d L }$ were tested in replicates of five on one Atellica CH Analyzer using three reagent lots over five days for a total of 125 determinations per lot. The maximum observed LoQ across all reagent lots was determined to be $0 . 0 4 ~ \mathrm { m g / d L }$ . The claimed LoQ is $0 . 1 \mathrm { m g / d L }$ which is the lowest concentration that met the performance goal of less than $20 \%$ CV.
