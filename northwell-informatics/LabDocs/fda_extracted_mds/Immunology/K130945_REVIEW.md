# L. Test Principle:

The test is performed on stool samples, collected without preservatives. The samples should be tested within 4 days, or frozen at ${ } - 2 0 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ if not immediately tested. An extract is prepared by combining the stool sample with extraction buffer at a ratio of 1:50 (e.g., $0 . 1 \ \mathrm { g m }$ of stool added with $4 . 9 \mathrm { m L }$ of extraction buffer) and mixing for 30 minutes. Following centrifugation, $2 0 \mu \mathrm { L }$ of the supernatant is diluted 1:50 with dilution buffer (final dilution 1:2500).

Calprest $^ \mathrm { \textregistered }$ is a colorimetric enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA). The assay uses a polyclonal rabbit antibody against calprotectin as the capture antibody. Calprotectin present in the diluted sample is bound by the antibody adsorbed onto the surface of the microtiter plate. Alkaline phosphatase conjugated antibodies (rabbit IgG antibodies) bind to the captured calprotectin. The enzyme catalyzes the conversion of the substrate to a colored product and the optical density (OD) of the sample is read on an ELISA plate reader. The intensity of the color is proportional to the amount of conjugate bound, and thus to the amount of captured calprotectin. The concentration of calprotectin in the extracted sample is interpreted from a standard curve generated from the 6 calibrators and converted to mg of calprotectin per $\mathrm { k g }$ of stool using a conversion of factor supplied by package insert.

Traceability: There is no international reference material for calprotectin. The calibrators and controls are manufactured using an approved reference lot which is traceable to the internal reference material cleared in k050007.

The calibrator S6, prepared from the master stock solution, was used to make the calibrators and controls. New lots of the calibrator set and controls were value assigned and validated by a comparison against an approved lot of calibrators and controls in three different assay runs and further testing with a panel of 12 internal quality control samples. Kit stability: The real time stability was performed using three lots of Calprest $^ \mathrm { \textregistered }$ kits (including calibrators and controls) stored under the recommended temperature at $2 -$ $8 \mathrm { { } ^ { \circ } C }$ . Data were collected at point 0, 6, 12, 18 and 19 months. The results support stability of the kits under the recommended storage of $2 - 8 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ for up to 18 months.

Open vial stability: The study was done to evaluate the reagent stability after opening. # d. Detection limit:

The limit of blank (LoB) was determined by assaying 5 blank samples 12 times. The LoB value was estimated to be $3 . 0 4 \mathrm { m g / k g }$ . The limit of detection (LoD) was determined by assaying 5 extracted stool samples with low calprotectin level 12 times. The LoD value was calculated as the $\mathrm { L o B } + 1 . 6 4 5 \mathrm { x } \mathrm { S D }$ of the replicates for the low level samples and was found to be $3 . 9 8 ~ \mathrm { { m g / k g } }$ .

The lower limit of the reportable assay range for Calprest $^ \mathrm { \textregistered }$ is $1 5 . 6 \mathrm { m g / k g }$ which corresponds to the lowest calibrator with a concentration of $6 . 2 5 ~ \mathrm { { n g / m L } }$ after conversion to mg of calprotectin per $\mathrm { k g }$ stool sample according to the assay’s instructions for use.

# e. Analytical specificity:

Interference studies were performed according to EP7-A2 using 5 pooled stool samples, each containing a mixture of 3 different stool samples. The pooled samples included 2 high positives (with calprotectin concentrations around $4 6 0 \mathrm { m g / k g }$ and 270 $\mathrm { m g / k g ) }$ , 1 low positive $( 1 4 5 \mathrm { m g / k g ) }$ , 1 sample within borderline range $( 6 8 \mathrm { m g / k g } )$ and 1 negative $( 3 0 \mathrm { m g / k g } )$ . For non-interference to be claimed, the $\%$ recovery of the spiked sample should be within $9 5 \mathrm { - } 1 0 5 \%$ of the neat sample. Stool samples were tested for potential interference by:

Microorganisms: The data demonstrated that Calprest $^ \mathrm { \textregistered }$ was not affected by Escherichia coli, Klebsiella pneumoniae, Salmonella spp., Shigella spp., and Yersinia spp. (at cell count of $1 . 5 \mathrm { x } 1 0 ^ { 7 }$ cfu/ml) in stool samples.

Drug and Nutrients: The data demonstrated that Calprest $^ \mathrm { \textregistered }$ was not affected by the following oral pharmaceuticals and nutritional supplements: Vancomycin (0.67 $\mathrm { m g } / 5 0 \mathrm { m g }$ stool); Ciprofloxacin HCL $( 0 . 5 0 \mathrm { m g } / 5 0 \mathrm { m g }$ stool); Prevacid $( 0 . 0 2 \mathrm { m g } / 5 0 $ mg stool); Azathioprine $( 0 . 0 7 \mathrm { m g } / 5 0 \mathrm { m g }$ stool); Prednisone $( 0 . 0 1 \mathrm { m g / 5 0 \mathrm { m g } }$ stool); Pentasa ( $1 . 3 3 \mathrm { m g } / 5 0 \mathrm { m g }$ stool); Asacol ( $1 . 3 3 \mathrm { m g / 5 0 m g }$ stool); multiple vitamin (Vit D: 1.1 UI/50 mg stool, Vit A: 8.0 UI/50 mg stool), Vit C: $0 . 0 5 ~ \mathrm { m g / 5 0 ~ m g }$ stool) and Vitamin E ( $0 . 1 0 \mathrm { m g } / 5 0 \mathrm { m g }$ stool).

Hemoglobin: The results showed that the addition of hemoglobin into the stool samples at level of $5 . 8 3 ~ \mathrm { m g / 5 0 ~ m g }$ stool did not interfere with Calprest $^ \mathrm { \textregistered }$ .

5. Expected values/Reference range:

The expected value in the normal healthy population is generally $< 5 0 \mathrm { m g / k g }$ according to study done by Røseth et al. $( 1 9 9 2 ) ^ { * }$ .

Ninety (90) samples from normal healthy people and 42 samples from patients with CD, UC, IBS, and IC were tested with Calprest $^ \mathrm { \textregistered }$ to validate the reference range value. The results showed that 85 out of 90 samples $( 9 2 . 2 \% )$ from normal healthy people tested negative and the remaining 5 samples fell in the range of $5 1 . 8 - 6 0 . 4 \mathrm { m g / k g }$ . Of the 42 samples from patients with CD, UC, IBS and IC, 36 $( 8 5 . 7 \% )$ tested positive by the assay.

\* Røseth AG, Fagerhol MK, Aadland E, Schonsby H. (1992). Assessment of the neutrophil-dominating protein calprotectin in feces. A methodology study. Scand J. Gastroenterol. 27(9): 793-798.
