# H. Intended Use:

1. Intended use(s):

Puritan OptiTranz Liquid Stuart Collection and Transport System is intended for use in the collection and transport of clinical specimen containing aerobic and fastidious bacteria from patient to the laboratory for bacteriological examination and culture.

3. Special conditions for use statement(s): For in vitro diagnostic use only For prescription use only

# I. Device Description:

Puritan OptiTranz Liquid Stuart Collection and Transport System is comprised of a sterile peel pouch containing a rayon or flock tipped swab applicator for collecting specimen and a polypropylene tube containing $1 ~ \mathrm { m l }$ of liquid Stuart medium.

Puritan OptiTranz Liquid Stuart Medium is a nonnutritive balanced salt solution containing phosphates to provide buffering capability and calcium chloride to provide essential ions that help maintain osmotic balance. Mercaptoacetic acid provides a reduced environment. It is recommended for maintaining the viability of aerobic and fastidious bacteria during the transport to the laboratory.

Approximate Puritan OptiTranz liquid Stuart medium formulation per liter

Calcium Chloride 0.1g Mercaptoacetic Acid 1.0g Sodium Glycerophosphate 10.0g

# M. Performance Characteristics (if/when applicable):

1. Analytical performance:

The performance characteristics of Puritan $^ \mathrm { \textregistered }$ OptiTranz Liquid Stuart Medium was determined using roll plate and swab elution methods out lined in The Clinical and Laboratory Standards Institute (CLSI) M40-A2 document. Rayon or flock tipped swabs designed for different applications were used. A variety of aerobic and one facultative organism were included in this study. The test organisms comprised of the ten ATCC strains five of which are recommended in the CLSI M40-A2 document for determining performance characteristics of swab transport systems. To determine the performance characteristics of Puritan OptiTranz Liquid Stuart Medium bacterial viability studies were performed. These studies were conducted under refrigerated $( 2 { - } 8 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C } )$ and room temperature $( 2 0 { - } 2 5 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C } )$ conditions. Rayon and flocked tipped swabs were inoculated in duplicate with specified volume of selective bacterial concentrations. These swabs were then placed in the transport vials containing Liquid Stuarts Media and held for 0, 24, and 48 hours; at the designated time intervals the swabs were removed and processed.

Organisms evaluated:

A. Aerobes 1. Pseudomonas aeruginosa ATCC BAA-427 2. Streptococcus pyogenes ATCC 19615 3. Streptococcus pneumoniae ATCC 6305 4. Haemophilus influenzae ATCC 10211   
B. Fastidious 1. Neisseria gonorrhoeae ATCC 43069.   
C. Additional organisms 1. Streptococcus agalactiae (Group B Strep) ATCC 13813, 2. Enterococcus faecalis(VRE) ATCC 51299, 3. Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA) ATCC 43300, 4. Bordetella parapertussis ATCC 15311 5. Bordetella pertussis ATCC 8467

Acceptance criteria for recovery of bacteria as recommended in the CLSI document M40-A2 are followed. Manual colony counts were conducted for the Roll-Plate Method and the Swab Elution Method at all three time intervals for each swab-organism combination, except Neisseria gonorrhoeae, and the average for each lot was recorded. Three dilutions were analyzed for each test organism and the dilution yielding zero-time colony counts between 94 to 300 CFU was reported. These viability results are acceptable.

Roll-Plate Method:

The results of the study by Roll-Plate Method are presented in Tables 1-4 below for 3 lots of Puritan OptiTranz Liquid Stuart Collection and Transport System. The results demonstrate that the Puritan OptiTranz Liquid Stuart Collection and Transport System were able to maintain acceptable viability of all organisms up to 48 hours at room temperature $( 2 0 - 2 5 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C } )$ and under refrigerated $\left( 4 \mathrm { - } 8 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C } \right)$ conditions based on the acceptance criteria in CLSI M40-A2. For the transport system to demonstrate acceptable viability by the Roll Plate method, ${ \ge } 5$ CFU should be recovered following the specified maintenance time from the specific dilution that yielded time-zero plate counts closest to 300 CFU. Neisseria gonorrhoeae results support acceptable recoveries up to $2 4 \mathrm { h }$ , as recommended in the CLSI guidance M40-A2. There is no CLSI M40-A2 interpretation for $N .$ . gonorrhoeae beyond $2 4 \mathrm { { h } }$ of storage using the Roll Plate Method.

Overgrowth Evaluation:

Viability performance studies on all of the organisms above also included an assessment of performance evaluation and overgrowth per CLSI M40‐A2 8.12.4 for roll plate method. Overgrowth assessment as defined in CLSI M40-A2 guideline is greater than one-log10 increase in CFU between zero-time and the holding time point. There was no increase in bacterial count for all organisms tested when the samples were stored at room temperature. The data provided is in conformance with the guidelines in CLSI M40-A2 at room temperature.
