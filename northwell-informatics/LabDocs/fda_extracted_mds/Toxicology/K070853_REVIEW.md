# L. Test Principle:

The ETOH method is based on an enzymatic reaction. Reagent 1 contains the buffering system. Reagent 2 contains alcohol dehydrogenase (ADH), the coenzyme nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide (NAD), buffer, preservatives, and stabilizers. The ADH catalyzes the oxidation of ethyl alcohol to acetaldehyde. During this reaction, NAD is reduced to NADH. The absorbance due to NADH (proportional to the ETOH concentration) is determined using a two-filter (340- $3 8 3 \mathrm { n m }$ ) bichromatic rate technique.

Ethyl alcohol $^ +$ NAD <--------> acetaldehyde $^ +$ NADH (absorbs at $3 4 0 \mathrm { n m }$ )

# a. Method comparison with predicate device:

Unaltered clinical patient serum and urine samples were used in the method comparison studies. The method comparison studies for 50 urine specimens were conducted at Dade Behring by R&D personnel.
