# H. Intended Use:

1. Intended use(s): See indications for use below.

2. Indication(s) for use:

For the quantitative measurement of acetaminophen in serum and plasma. Measurement of acetaminophen is used in the diagnosis and treatment of acetaminophen overdose toxicity. Excessive amounts of acetaminophen leads to hepatotoxicity and nephrotoxicity. In acute overdosage, acetaminophen can cause severe hepatic damage leading to hepatic failure if untreated.

3. Special conditions for use statement(s): For prescription use only.

4. Special instrument requirements: Performance characteristics were established using the Hitachi 717 analyzer.

# I. Device Description:

This device contains three liquid ready-to-use components; a component that contains an acetaminophen enzyme reagent, an acetaminophen color reagent, and an acetaminophen standard.

# L. Test Principle:

The enzyme, acyl amidohydrolase, cleaves the amide bond of the acetaminophen molecule, leaving p-aminophenol and acetate. The p-aminophenol is coupled with 2,5-dimethylphenol in the presence of manganese ions to form a colored compound, 4-(4-iminophenol)-2,5-dimethylcyclohexadiene-1-one. The increased absorbance due to the formation of 4-(4-iminophenol)-2,5-dimethylcylcohexadiene-1-one is directly proportional to the acetaminophen in the sample.

# b. Linearity/assay reportable range:

Linearity was evaluated on the Hitachi 717 by testing a spiked serum set that consisted of 11 concentrations of acetaminophen across the assay range. Each sample was tested four times and linearity was calculated. All samples tested were within $\pm 3 . 9 \%$ of the expected value and the linear regression calculations resulted in a slope of 0.993 and an intercept of $1 . 6 \mu \mathrm { m o l / L }$ . These results support the manufacture’s claimed measurement range of $4 { - } 2 5 0 0 \ \mathrm { u m o l / L }$ .

c. Traceability, Stability, Expected values (controls, calibrators, or methods):

The calibrator was previously cleared under k952949.

# d. Detection limit:

The limit of quantitation (LoQ) was evaluated on the Hitachi 717 by performing serial dilutions of a commercial control, then testing each dilution $\scriptstyle \mathrm { n = 4 0 }$ over 5 days. The sponsor defined the LoQ as the lowest concentration at which the $C V \%$ did not exceed $20 \%$ . The LoQ was calculated as $4 ~ { \mu \mathrm { m o l / L } }$ , supporting the claimed lower limit of the assay.

2. Comparison studies:

a. Method comparison with predicate device:

A method comparison study was performed on the Hitachi 717 by testing 88 patient samples in singlicate on the Acetaminophen L3K Assay and a previously cleared predicate device. The samples ranged in concentration from 38 to $2 3 6 1 ~ \mathrm { \mu m o l / L }$ . Linear regression was performed and resulted in a slope of 1.063 ( $9 5 \%$ confidence interval 1.058 to 1.068), a y-intercept of 7.0 $\mu \mathrm { m o l / L }$ $9 5 \%$ confidence interval 3.2 to 11.2), and a correlation coefficient of 0.9998.

# $b$ . Matrix comparison:

A matrix comparison study was performed on the Hitachi 717 by testing 25 sets of matched serum and lithium heparin plasma samples with acetaminophen concentrations that spanned the full measuring range of the device. Linear regression analysis was performed and resulted in the following equation. $\mathrm { y } = 0 . 9 9 9 \mathrm { x } - 2 . 2$ . The correlation coefficient was 0.9999.
