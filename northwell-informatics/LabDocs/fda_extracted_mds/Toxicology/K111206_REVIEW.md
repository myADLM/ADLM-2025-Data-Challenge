# H. Intended Use:

1. Intended use(s): Refer to Indications for Use below

2. Indication(s) for use:

The Teco Alcohol Saliva Test is a screening test for the semi-quantitative detection of the presence of ethyl alcohol in human saliva. The test strip indicates relative Blood Alcohol Concentrations (BAC) at $0 . 0 \%$ , $0 . 0 2 \%$ , $0 . 0 4 \%$ , $0 . 0 8 \%$ , and $0 . 3 0 \%$ cut-off levels. Results are used for the diagnosis of alcohol intoxication. The test is intended for both prescription and over-the-counter in vitro diagnostic use. The assay is a disposable test for one-time use.

3. Special conditions for use statement(s): The device is single use and disposable

4. Special instrument requirements: None – each device is single use and self-contained

# I. Device Description:

The Saliva Alcohol Reagent Strips consist of an alcohol reagent pad affixed onto a firm plastic strip. Cutoffs available are 0.00, 0.02, 0.04, 0.08, and $0 . 3 \%$ BAC. There will be one test strip packaged into one foil pouch.

# L. Test Principle:

The test principle of the strip is based on coupled enzymatic reactions that take place on the pad to visualize the BAC. The first reaction is an oxidation reaction where the ethanol in the saliva is coupled with alcohol oxidase and converted into an aldehyde and peroxide. In the second reaction, tetramethylbenzidine (TMB) is oxidized with the peroxide from the first reaction, with peroxidase acting as the catalyst. TMB is the color indicator substrate, and the degree of color change corresponds to the amount of ethanol present in the saliva.

c. Traceability, Stability, Expected values (controls, calibrators, or methods):

The sponsor states that the cutoffs are verified during manufacturing using ethanol solutions with known concentrations verified by a clinical chemistry analyzer.

# d. Detection limit:

To assess the possibility of false positives, the sponsor performed a study analyzing alcohol-free ( $0 \%$ BAC) saliva samples. Each of 10 samples was analyzed 10 times for a total n of 100. There were no false positives observed when measuring zero concentration samples.

# e. Analytical specificity:

The sponsor performed studies to evaluate the effect of substances which may be present in the saliva sample as well as a range of lighting and temperature conditions.

# Substances that are consumed or used orally:

The sponsor evaluated the effect of cigarette smoke, chewing tobacco, mouthwash, chewing gum, cough syrup, and breath spray on the assay. For each potential interferent, samples were collected from ten volunteers, of which five were drinkers and five were non-drinkers. Results were read at 1 minute and 10 minutes after consumption or use of the potential interferents. Samples were read by untrained laypersons. There were no false positives or false negatives when samples were read at 10 minutes after use or consumption of the potential interferent. False positives were observed for cigarette smoke, chewing tobacco, and mouthwash when read at one minute. The labeling specifies that users should not consume anything or have anything in the mouth for at least ten minutes prior to use.

# Volatile Substances:

The sponsor conducted a study to assess the potential for positive or negative interference from volatile substances. The volatile substance was spiked into alcohol-free saliva samples and samples with alcohol concentrations of 0.02, 0.04, 0.08, and $0 . 3 0 \%$ BAC. No interference was seen for acetone, 2-butanol, glycerol, or isopropanol at the concentrations listed below.

2. Comparison studies:

a. Method comparison with predicate device:

The sponsor conducted a consumer study comparing their device to results from the predicate Mission Saliva Alcohol Test Strip.

The study included 25 participants designated as the alcohol-drinkers and 25 naïve non-alcohol drinkers designated as the readers. Each drinker provided a negative saliva sample and then consumed varying amounts of alcohol after which saliva samples were tested at 10 minute intervals for a total of 6 samples per drinker. A total of 20 additional samples were spiked with alcohol to achieve concentrations $> 0 . 0 8 \%$ BAC. Therefore a total of 195 strips were read for this study. All of the reacted strips were randomized and blinded before being presented to the readers. After the study the participants completed a questionnaire regarding the ease of use and interpretation of results.
