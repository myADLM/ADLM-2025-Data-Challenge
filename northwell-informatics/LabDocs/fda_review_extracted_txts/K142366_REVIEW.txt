I. Device Description:
Puritan OptiTranz Liquid Stuart Collection and Transport System is comprised of a sterile
peel pouch containing a rayon or flock tipped swab applicator for collecting specimen and a
polypropylene tube containing 1 ml of liquid Stuart medium.
Puritan OptiTranz Liquid Stuart Medium is a nonnutritive balanced salt solution containing
phosphates to provide buffering capability and calcium chloride to provide essential ions that
help maintain osmotic balance. Mercaptoacetic acid provides a reduced environment. It is
recommended for maintaining the viability of aerobic and fastidious bacteria during the
transport to the laboratory.
Approximate Puritan OptiTranz liquid Stuart medium formulation per liter
Calcium Chloride 0.1g
Mercaptoacetic Acid 1.0g
Sodium Glycerophosphate 10.0g

determined using roll plate and swab elution methods out lined in The Clinical and
Laboratory Standards Institute (CLSI) M40-A2 document. Rayon or flock tipped swabs
designed for different applications were used. A variety of aerobic and one facultative
organism were included in this study. The test organisms comprised of the ten ATCC strains
five of which are recommended in the CLSI M40-A2 document for determining performance
characteristics of swab transport systems. To determine the performance characteristics of
Puritan OptiTranz Liquid Stuart Medium bacterial viability studies were performed. These

Elution Method at all three time intervals for each swab-organism combination, except
Neisseria gonorrhoeae, and the average for each lot was recorded. Three dilutions were
analyzed for each test organism and the dilution yielding zero-time colony counts between 94
to 300 CFU was reported. These viability results are acceptable.
Roll-Plate Method:
The results of the study by Roll-Plate Method are presented in Tables 1-4 below for 3 lots of
Puritan OptiTranz Liquid Stuart Collection and Transport System. The results demonstrate
that the Puritan OptiTranz Liquid Stuart Collection and Transport System were able to
maintain acceptable viability of all organisms up to 48 hours at room temperature (20-25°C)
and under refrigerated (4-8°C) conditions based on the acceptance criteria in CLSI M40-A2.
For the transport system to demonstrate acceptable viability by the Roll Plate method, ≥5
CFU should be recovered following the specified maintenance time from the specific dilution
that yielded time-zero plate counts closest to 300 CFU. Neisseria gonorrhoeae results
support acceptable recoveries up to 24 h, as recommended in the CLSI guidance M40-A2.
There is no CLSI M40-A2 interpretation for N. gonorrhoeae beyond 24 h of storage using
the Roll Plate Method.

Overgrowth Evaluation:
Viability performance studies on all of the organisms above also included an assessment of
performance evaluation and overgrowth per CLSI M40‐A2 8.12.4 for roll plate method.
Overgrowth assessment as defined in CLSI M40-A2 guideline is greater than one-log10
increase in CFU between zero-time and the holding time point. There was no increase in
bacterial count for all organisms tested when the samples were stored at room temperature.
The data provided is in conformance with the guidelines in CLSI M40-A2 at room

were observed.
